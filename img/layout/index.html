About Spectre Fleet

In EVE there are many diplomatic doctrines. These diplomatic doctrines are used mainly to prevent diplomatic incidents among friendly or neutral corporations, alliances and coalitions. But what if you don't want to think about repercussions whenever you decide to shoot somebody? What if you want to experience PVP as if you weren't in a corporation, and everybody is a target? What if you just really want to give diplomacy the finger? Then NPSI is the diplomatic doctrine for you. And Spectre Fleet is where you experience NPSI to the fullest.

Spectre Fleet is the biggest and baddest public NPSI (Not Purple Shoot It) community in EVE Online. The channel 'Spectre Fleet', the place our participants meet up for fleets, is visited by hundreds every day. Our fleets, of which we have multiple every day, depart from various trade hubs throughout the universe. Most notably Amarr in the Amarr Throne Worlds Constellation.

"Although not new to EVE, the concept of 'public fleets' is becoming increasingly popular, and is a concept most notably demonstrated by Spectre Fleet. Public fleets are, as their title implies, fleets open up to everyone with no strings attached. Pilots decide on a doctrine, form up, and go searching for a fight against anything that they can find." by TheMittani.com

"Spectre Fleets are run frequently from the in-game channel “Spectre Fleet”. Players simply join the channel and either organize or join up with a fleet that's being put together. There are no API checks, interviews, or previous experience required to join. When an FC decides to start a fleet, they advertise in the channel what type of doctrine they want to use and where they're going to look for fights. Anyone who is interested simply x's up and they'll receive a fleet invite." by CCP Eterne

Recent Activities

The Storm of Kamela A battle raging between multiple large fleets, in which 50 billion ISK has been destroyed. "The Kamela system, located in The Bleak Lands and the Amarr-Minmatar warzone, is well known for its close proximity to the Amarr trade hub, as well as the normal frigate skirmishes native to all Faction Warfare. However, if you have been traveling through Kamela this past Sunday, on September 28th, you would have seen an much different sight than expected. Such was the case for travelers who happened to pass through the warzone's most southern system, only to witness an epic slugfest complete with both triage carriers and TiDi."

The Aldali Project How a single Spectre FC killed a super carrier. With a little help of his friends. "Ninja led a fleet, nothing. He was returning to Amarr after brawling with a carrier that got away when a quiet ping arrived: 'super is back'. He'd gotten so caught up in chasing the carrier that the watch list green flash had eluded him completely. Now, even a spider cannot bring down a behemoth without friends. Ninja called on other FC's from Spectre Fleet, trusting in some of the best NPSI has to offer. Prober? Check. Two HICs? Check. The Aldali Project was a go."

A Roam with Spectre Fleet "Established in January 2014, Spectre Fleet FC Ethien Green explained in an interview with TMC how Spectre Fleet was founded after Bombers Bar, another public fleet, experienced a decline in activity due to leadership changes. Jayne Fillon and Nonnak Severin cofounded Spectre Fleet with the help of several FCs from Bombers Bar and expanded the scope of their fleet operations. While Bombers Bar focused solely on covert ops fleets, Spectre Fleet differentiates itself by maintaining no restrictions on what types of fleets it runs. For instance, in any 1-2 week span, Spectre Fleet features small frigate roams, black ops fleets, battlecruiser and battleship brawling fleets; some fleets even feature capital ship support. With a couple hundred pilots connected to the in-game channel 'Spectre Fleet,' the size of each operation varies from a handful to well over 100."

What we fly

In Spectre Fleet, we fly many different doctrines. Ranging from frigate roams to battleship combat. From classical doctrines such as the Baltec doctrine to experimental doctrines such as hulltanked Brutix fleets.

New and old players alike are welcome to join in every fleet. Even if you cannot fly a doctrine ship, the fleet commander will find a suitable role for you.

Our Fleet Commanders

The people providing access to fun

Spectre Fleet Commanders (FCs) are divided into several ranks. There are the commanders, the effective directors/administrators of the Fleet. Then there's the full Fleet Commanders, who take out fleets regularly and have proven themselves to be competent at leading NPSI fleets. Lastly, there are the Junior FCs (Jr. FC). Spectre FCs come from a variety of corporations and alliances throughout EVE. From major Sovblock alliances to smaller elite PVP groups, Spectre Fleet Commanders are varied and above all else, skilled.
